Drevenmüir Rvun

Drevenmüir Rvun is the language spoken by the people living on the Drevenmüir Penninsula. It is split into 3 dialects: Standard Drevenmüir, Southern Drevenmüir, and Low Drevenmüir. In addition, many important texts are still written in the literary dialect of Classical Drevenmüir.

Phrases to know for travellers!

  1. Gnacht elen - Hello
  2. De zai ölen - Goodbye
  3. Ze älojje elen - I'm sorry
  4. Schige dwä - Thank you
  5. Zer rvojjej - You're welcome
  6. Rvöuv pföu zacge dwä? - How much does that cost?
  7. Ze rvöuv zalscheñ dwä? - May I have that?
  8. LOCATION pföus ere? - Where is LOCATION?

Demographic and ethnographic information

The Drevenmüir dialects of Standard and Southern are spoken by the Dwarves living in Drevenmüir. Standard Drevenmüir is predominantly spoken by the Dwarves living in and near the Hrkerghrgän

Sentence final particles

The particle che marks a sentence is a question.

De Drevenmüir Rvuñ rwunch che?
2asg.NOM Drevenmüir DEF.Language.ACC speak-2asg.PRS IM
Do you speak Drevenmüirish?

The particle dwä is mostly used to make a sentence more polite, similar to "please" in English. Unlike English, this particle can be used at the end of most sentences. If the verb of the clause is in the future tense, the meaning changes. In this case, the particle would be used to ask someone to do something for the subject of the sentence.

Ze rvöuv zalscheñ dwä?
1asg.NOM IN-one.ACC have-1asg.FUT PROP
May I have that?
